Why i started coding and when i knew it was for me.

I started this journey just over a year ago now and i have to say its one of the best decisions i ever made. I always loved to be creative, whether it was 3D modelling using blender, making silver jewellery or just finding new ways to do things.

When i discovered coding it started from my love of video games and thinking "I wonder if i could make something like this?, Any feature or game mechanic i wanted?! Sign me up!"

So naturally i started with C++ and learnt to love it, creating algorithims for basic things like a game of tic-tac-toe or a cash machine thats nice to you if you have loads of money..weird i know but i was having fun learning the process.

A lot of video, reading and discoveries later i was left spoilt for choice. "There's how many languages?!" "Wait there's a front AND a back end?" "Hang on you're telling me there's libraries that are NOT full of books?.."

And that's when it happened.. i caught the bug, reaslising there was endless possibility to what i could make and create, i just needed to gain the knowledge and i wanted to do it quickly.

Gaining the knowledge i needed... maybe?

Once i realised i wanted to pursue development as a career i looked for online courses to vary my skills. I did Cisco's C++ associate course and then found out i didnt have the money to pay for the final exam. After, i found FreeCodeCamp and completed their certification in Responsive Web Development that covered HTML5,CSS3 and basic JavaScript.

During all of the free online courses i knew they would help me show initiative and interest but i couldn't help but feel like they weren't going to be credible enough to help me get an apprenticeship or entry-level job so i started to look elsewhere.

I tried my luck with apprenticeships, "A-level or equivalent in a STEM background OR a level 3 software development diploma". Okay so i used to be a vehicle technician but that doesnt REALLY count and i thought the idea of an apprenticeship was to help me get those qualifications?

My background, what i did before and why im here.

As i mentioned breifly above what i mainly used to do was repair vehicles, i prefer "vehicle technician" over "mechanic" but thats mainly due to the stigma around the title. Anyway, i started at Porsche which sounds fancy i know and it felt it at times when you were trusted with your next job on a $1million+ supercar but the role definetely lacked creative freedom.

A redundancy later and i found a new job across the road *literally* at Ford, same deal, less fancy. A second redundancy and a rather stubborn pandemic later i found myself out of a job with no trust in the motor trade, i needed something reliable, something to keep me going whilst i studied. Thats it, ill get a job in retail, boost my people skills during the day and study in the evenings, retail is booming, always has been.. right?

Well, after just over a year.. you guessed it.. redundancy number 3 *no prizes for guessing that one though* the company closed just over 200 stores and i decided to open up to new possibilities sooner rather than later. I had saved just enough money to survive so i made the decision to study full time and go after what i REALLY wanted.

Why i think i am suited to the role.

Although my past work experience doesn't exactly scream "i'd be a great developer" it has certainly given me skills that would be useful. My time at Porsche gave me the ability to work amazingly in a team whilst also being able to put my head down and get work done solo. At Ford i learnt the importance of following processes when they're needed and undertanding the importance of not being afraid to ask questions, even if they seem silly.

Retail certainly taught me alot but what i got from it the most was a major boost to my people skills, a much more professional mindset and a huge appreciation for customer/colleague relationships.

The decision to study full-time and use my savings along with the 3 redundancies may seem bad but i wouldnt be here without them. They taught me not to give up, gave me experience in "working from home" and allowed me to find out i enjoy hours spent infront of my PC learning and creating.

What i'm looking for and want to achieve.

Ultimately i am looking for an apprenticeship or entry-level position in either software development or web development.

I understand my skills and credentials aren't exactly incredible but ive gained considerable knowledge in a short space of time. I am super passionate about surrounding myself with the enviroment and resources i need to constantly improve and level-up my skills.

A role with training, room for progression and plenty of projects to get stuck into would be ideal. But i am 100% open to other ideas and roles part-time or full-time.
